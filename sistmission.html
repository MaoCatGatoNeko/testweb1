MISSION STATEMENT:

To thrive as an independent bookstore by offering the best service and the deepest, most diverse choice of books. sidelines, and services; To promote gender and racial equality and fight homophobia in our selection of books and in all elements of the store environment, from the images on the wall, to the music we play, to the attitudes of our staff; To demonstrate the diversity as well as the commonality of women's experience by providing a wide range of multi-cultural, lesbian, and bi materials, as well as titles on every aspect of women's lives and history; To provide a center for the women's community where women and men can gather, exchange information, and use our resources to move the community towards political action.

BEGINNINGS

SISTERHOOD BOOKSTORE was founded in 1972 by three women active in the Los Angeles movement for women's Iiberation. Two of the founders, Adele Wallace and Simone Wallace. continue to own and operate the store with the support of an enthusiastic, knowledgeable staff. The bookstore began as a gathering place and distribution center for the phenomenal outpouring of books, magazines, and artistic and political projects emerging from a heightened women's consciousness. Sisterhood soon became the center for information, resources, and the lively exchange of progressive ideas that it remains today. Throughout the liberal 70's, the conservative 80's, and the volatile 90's, Sisterhood continues to be a rewarding haven for anyone who wishes to experience the richness and diversity of the community of women
